Iodine (I)53

Reactive Nonmetal

Iodine was discovered by accident. In 1811 chemist Bernard Courtois used too much sulfuric acid in the process of extracting saltpeter from seaweed. Violet vapor appeared and condensed into black crystals which he called "substance X".

Symbol: I

Atomic mass: 126.90447 u

Density (when liquid at melting point): 4.933 g/cm3

Melting: 113.7 °C

Boiling: 184.3 °C

Discovered by: Bernard Courtois

Year of discovery: 1811
