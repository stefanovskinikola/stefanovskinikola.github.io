Samarium (Sm)62

Lanthanide

The major commercial application of samarium is in samarium–cobalt magnets, which have permanent magnetization second only to neodymium magnets.

Symbol: Sm

Atomic mass: 150.36 u

Density (when liquid at melting point): 7.16 g/cm3

Melting: 1072 °C

Boiling: 1793.85 °C

Discovered by: Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran, Eugène-Anatole Demarçay

Year of discovery: 1879
