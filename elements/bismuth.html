Bismuth (Bi)83

Post-Transition Metal

The variations in the thickness of the oxide layer that forms on the surface of the bismuth crystal cause different wavelengths of light to interfere upon reflection, thus displaying a rainbow of colors.

Symbol: Bi

Atomic mass: 208.9804 u

Density (when liquid at melting point): 10.05 g/cm3

Melting: 271.5 °C

Boiling: 1564 °C

Discovered by: Claude François Geoffroy

Year of discovery: 1753
